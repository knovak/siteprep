Overview

Udaipur

Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur is framed by the Aravalli hills and dotted with ornate palaces and havelis along Lake Pichola. It is a quieter Rajasthan escape with strong craft traditions.

Population: ~0.6 million (city)
Typical January weather: 8–23°C, crisp mornings
Typical February weather: 11–26°C, clear skies and comfortable afternoons

Fast facts

  • Highlights: Palace-lined lakes, island gardens, and hilltop sunset viewpoints.
  • Markets: Handicrafts, miniature paintings, and silverwork around Hathi Pol and Bada Bazaar.
  • Transit: Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR) is ~22 km east; taxis and autos cover most local trips.
  • Best times: Winter months for lake views and rooftop dining without heat haze.

Jawai leopard safari

Jawai/Bera leopard safari trips are among the most reliable big-cat sightings in India, with 100+ leopards recorded in open granite terrain and waterholes that boost visibility in cooler months. Wildlife viewing can also include crocodiles, hyenas, deer, and 150+ bird species on a good drive.

Shared or solo safari rides typically start around ₹2,750+ per person for short drives and can reach ₹5,000+ per person for private gypsy tours, depending on season and operator. Camp packages vary widely: mid-range tents or lodges with meals and safari add-ons often run ₹6,000–₹15,000+ per night, while higher luxury stays go beyond that range.

Bagore-ki-Haveli Dharohar folk dance

Each evening, Dharohar Folk Dance stages an hour-long cultural show in the courtyard of an 18th-century haveli on Lake Pichola. Traditional Rajasthani performances include Ghoomar, Terah Taali, and Bhavai balancing acts, plus puppet theatre and live music beneath glowing stone balconies.

Showtime is typically around 7–8 PM daily, with tickets often sold starting near 6:15 PM on a first-come basis. Arrive 30–60 minutes early to secure better seating, and check Bagore-ki-Haveli dance show updates for current timing and pricing.

Heritage walks & lakefront logistics

  • Most heritage walks thread together Jagdish Chowk, Jagdish Temple, the central bazaar, City Palace precincts, and the lakeside ghats with stories of Mewar royalty, crafts, and street life.
  • Lake Pichola Municipal Boat Ride Point runs roughly 09:00 to 17:00 with a separate sunset cruise window, so aim for late afternoon to catch golden light.
  • Manshapurn Karni Mata Ropeway Udaipur runs roughly 09:00–~21:00 (last cabin around 21:00), with hour-slot bookings on the official ticket page; plan 1–2 hours for the ride, temple stop, and viewpoints above Lake Pichola.
