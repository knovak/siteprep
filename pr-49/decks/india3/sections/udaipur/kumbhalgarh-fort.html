Attraction

Kumbhalgarh Fort (UNESCO Hill Fort)

Udaipur

Kumbhalgarh Fort is a UNESCO-listed hill fort famous for its massive wall and dramatic Aravalli views, often visited as a day trip from Udaipur.

History & context

Constructed largely in the 15th century by Rana Kumbha, the fort formed a strategic Mewar stronghold with more than 300 temples inside its walls.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Typically 9:00 AM–6:00 PM; a light-and-sound show is often held in the evening.
  • Address: Kumbhalgarh Fort, Rajsamand district, Rajasthan 313325.
  • Google Map: Kumbhalgarh Fort, Rajsamand district, Rajasthan 313325
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: About 85 km northwest of Udaipur (roughly 2–2.5 hours by road).

What travelers like

  • Impressive scale and UNESCO status.
  • Long stretches of fort wall for panoramic viewpoints.
  • Less crowded than some major forts.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Long day-trip drive from Udaipur.
  • Limited shade inside the fort complex.
  • Public transport is sparse—best by taxi.
