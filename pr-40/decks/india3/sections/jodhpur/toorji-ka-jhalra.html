Attraction

Toorji Ka Jhalra Bavdi (Stepwell)

Jodhpur

Toorji Ka Jhalra is an 18th-century stepwell tucked into the old city, now a popular spot for architecture lovers and café breaks.

History & context

Commissioned by Maharaja Abhay Singh's queen in the 1740s, the stepwell once served as a vital water source and community gathering point.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Open daily; best visited in daylight hours for safety and photos.
  • Address: Toorji Ka Jhalra, Makrana Mohalla, Jodhpur, Rajasthan 342001.
  • Google Map: Toorji Ka Jhalra, Makrana Mohalla, Jodhpur, Rajasthan 342001
  • Distance from Jodhpur city center: About 0.5 km from the Clock Tower and Sardar Market.

What travelers like

  • Striking geometric steps and stone carvings.
  • Quiet courtyard feel despite the central location.
  • Great photo spot in softer morning light.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Water levels can be low outside monsoon.
  • Uneven steps require care.
  • Can feel crowded as cafés nearby fill up.
