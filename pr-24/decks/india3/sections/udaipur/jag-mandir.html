Attraction

Jag Mandir (Lake Pichola Island Palace)

Udaipur

Jag Mandir is an island palace on Lake Pichola known for its marble pavilions, gardens, and historic role as a royal retreat.

History & context

Built mainly in the 17th century under Maharana Jagat Singh I, Jag Mandir is celebrated for its Mughal-influenced architecture and its use as a refuge for Prince Khurram (later Shah Jahan).

Visitor details

  • Hours: Typically 10:00 AM–6:00 PM; access is only by boat.
  • Address: Jag Mandir Island, Lake Pichola, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001.
  • Google Map: Google Map
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: About 1–2 km from the lakefront by boat; access via City Palace/Lal Ghat docks.

What travelers like

  • Marble pavilions and lake-facing gardens.
  • Quiet island atmosphere away from the old-city lanes.
  • Great photo angles back toward the City Palace.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Requires a separate boat ticket and timing around departures.
  • Limited shade during midday.
  • Café and refreshments are limited.
