Attraction

Jagdish Temple

Udaipur

Jagdish Temple is Udaipur's largest Hindu temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu (as Jagannath) and known for its intricate carvings and evening aarti rituals.

History & context

The temple was completed in 1651 by Maharana Jagat Singh and remains an active place of worship with richly carved exterior reliefs and a multi-story shikhara.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Open early morning and evening (typically around 4:30 AM–1:00 PM and 5:00–8:30 PM).
  • Address: Jagdish Temple Road, Old City, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001.
  • Google Map: Google Map
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: Around 1 km from the Clock Tower and central bazaar.

What travelers like

  • Detailed stone carvings and the main sanctum.
  • Atmospheric evening aarti with bells and chanting.
  • Easy stop while walking the old city.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Steep steps at the entrance.
  • Crowded during prayer times.
  • Limited quiet zones for longer stays.
