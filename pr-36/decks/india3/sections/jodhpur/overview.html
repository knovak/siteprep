Overview

Jodhpur

Jodhpur, the Blue City, rises from the Thar Desert with Mehrangarh Fort towering over indigo-painted lanes and bustling stepwells. It blends royal history with desert adventure routes.

Population: ~1.5 million (metro)
Typical January weather: 9–23°C, dry and sunny
Typical February weather: 12–27°C, warm afternoons

Fast facts

  • Highlights: Fort-top vistas, blue-painted lanes, and market bazaars in the old city core.
  • Food: Mirchi vada and mawa kachori are local favorites; rooftop cafes offer fort views.
  • Transit: Jodhpur Airport (JDH) sits close to town; trains link to Delhi, Jaipur, and Ahmedabad.
  • Desert access: Osian and Thar dune excursions start from the city.

Advice

  • Most packaged tours from Jodhpur include mid-afternoon hotel pickup, transport to Osian, camel and/or jeep safari time, dinner with entertainment, and a return to the city by around 9–10 PM.
  • Jodhpur morning heritage walk (2–2.5 hrs): start at the Clock Tower (Ghanta Ghar), wind through the blue alleys and markets to Toorji Ka Jhalra (18th-century stepwell), and finish near Sardar Market for crowd-free photos before shops open.
