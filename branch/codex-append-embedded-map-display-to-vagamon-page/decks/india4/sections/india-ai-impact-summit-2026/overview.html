Overview

India AI Impact Summit 2026

The India AI Impact Summit 2026 is a Government of India-led gathering focused on scaling responsible AI across policy, research, industry, and public engagement. This page covers your on-the-ground window in Delhi (Feb 17–22) and pulls highlights from the published summit agenda.

Official summit dates: Feb 16–20, 2026
Your on-site window: Feb 17–22, 2026
Primary hub: Bharat Mandapam (Pragati Maidan)

Purpose + themes

  • Mission: amplify the positive impact of AI through policy alignment, research collaboration, and industry deployment.
  • Foundational pillars: People, Planet, and Progress (the summit’s three “Sutras”).
  • Focus areas: the agenda is organized into seven “Chakras” that map AI use cases and partnerships across sectors.

Daily highlights (Feb 17–22)

Tue, Feb 17

  • Purpose: knowledge-sharing day with sector compendiums and applied AI sessions.
  • Highlights: releases of AI compendiums (health, energy, education, agriculture, gender empowerment, accessibility), applied AI seminar, and “AI by HER: Global Impact Challenge.”
  • Locations: Bharat Mandapam; Sushma Swaraj Bhawan; Ambedkar Bhawan (Dr. Ambedkar International Centre).

Wed, Feb 18

  • Purpose: research + industry focus with deep-dive technical sessions.
  • Highlights: research symposium, industry session, continued “AI by HER” challenge, plus keynotes, panels, and roundtables.
  • Locations: Bharat Mandapam; Sushma Swaraj Bhawan; Ambedkar Bhawan.

Thu, Feb 19

  • Purpose: leadership programming and executive engagement.
  • Highlights: opening ceremony, leaders’ plenary, CEO roundtable, plus keynotes, panels, and roundtables.
  • Locations: Bharat Mandapam; Sushma Swaraj Bhawan; Ambedkar Bhawan. Summit dinner noted at the Convention Centre.

Fri, Feb 20

  • Purpose: global coordination and wrap-up sessions.
  • Highlights: GPAI Council Meeting plus keynotes, panels, and roundtables.
  • Locations: Bharat Mandapam; Sushma Swaraj Bhawan; Ambedkar Bhawan.

Sat–Sun, Feb 21–22

  • Purpose: no official summit programming listed; reserve for follow-up meetings, private demos, or Delhi excursions.
  • Highlights: buffer time for outbound meetings or exploring Old Delhi, Lodhi Garden, or the National Museum.
  • Locations: self-scheduled across Delhi.

Registered attendee notes

  • Since you’re already registered: plan to arrive early on Feb 17 for badge pickup, security checks, and day-one briefing packets.
  • Daily rhythm: expect overlapping keynotes/panels across venues—pick a primary track each morning and lock meeting slots for afternoon breaks.
