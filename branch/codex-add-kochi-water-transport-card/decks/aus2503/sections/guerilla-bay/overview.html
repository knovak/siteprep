Overview

Guerilla Bay

Guerilla Bay is a quiet south coast enclave just north of Batemans Bay, known for its rock-fringed beaches, bushwalking tracks, and resident kangaroos grazing by the dunes.

The bay sits on Yuin Country, close to the Murramarang Aboriginal Area where cultural connections to the coast continue today. The name “Guerilla Bay” is commonly linked to early coastal charts referencing a 19th-century vessel called the Guerilla, reflecting the area’s maritime history.

Region: Eurobodalla, New South Wales south coast
Drive time: ~10 minutes north of Batemans Bay, ~2 hours east of Canberra
Typical March weather: 18–25°C, warm days, cooler evenings, occasional coastal showers

Hiking opportunities

  • Guerilla Bay headland walk: Short sandy and rocky paths link the main beach, smaller coves, and lookouts with sweeping ocean views.
  • Depot Beach coastal track: Follow the Murramarang National Park shoreline for a longer stroll between bays and picnic spots.
  • Pebbly Beach loop: Combine forest and beachfront paths to spot wildlife and take in the dramatic coastline.

Nearby highlights

  • Murramarang National Park: Beach-to-bush trails with wallabies and ocean lookouts.
  • Depot Beach: Sheltered bay with calm surf and picnic areas.
  • Batemans Bay: Waterfront dining, oyster farms, and kayak hires on the Clyde River.
  • Malua Bay: Family-friendly swimming and snorkelling spots.

March planning tips

  • Pack light layers for cooler nights and sea breezes after sunset.
  • Expect generally calm seas, but check local surf reports before swimming.
  • Bring reef-safe sunscreen and insect repellent for dusk walks.
