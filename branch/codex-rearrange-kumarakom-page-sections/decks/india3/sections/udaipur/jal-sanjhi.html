Jal Sanjhi (Water-Painting Ritual)

Udaipur

Jal Sanjhi is a seasonal ritual art form where intricate stencil designs are floated on water, often dedicated to Krishna and celebrated in local temples.

History & context

The tradition is associated with devotional practices in northern India and is observed in Udaipur at the Radha Vallabh Temple during late monsoon and early autumn festivals.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Typically observed in the evening during the seasonal festival period; exact timings vary by lunar calendar.
  • Address: Radha Vallabh Temple, Old City, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001.
  • Google Map: Radha Vallabh Temple, Old City, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: About 1 km from the old city market and City Palace area.
