Thaipooyam Festival

Haripad, Alappuzha · Sunday Feb 1, 2026

Thaipooyam Mahotsavam is a devotional Murugan festival known for kavadi processions, ritual offerings, and percussion-led celebrations across Kerala temple towns.

History

Thaipooyam honors Lord Subrahmanya (Murugan) and is rooted in devotional narratives of victory and protection. The Haripad celebration centers on the Sree Subrahmanya Swamy Temple — one of Kerala's oldest Murugan shrines, sometimes called Dakshina Palani (Wikipedia).

In 2026 the festival window is January 30–February 1, with the peak day on Sunday, February 1 (Thai month, Poosam star) per Drik Panchang and temple programme notes.

Programme highlights on Feb 1: morning Aana Ezhunnellippu (elephant procession) and mela performances ~07:00–09:00; evening 17:00 procession with Talapoli and Deepalankara Ratham (chariot).

Music

Festival soundscapes are led by mela (percussion orchestras) and chenda ensembles accompanying kavadi bearers and processions. Expect chenda, maddalam, ilathalam, kombu, and kuzhal with intensity building toward climactic crescendos — one of the defining experiences of Thaipooyam in Haripad (templesofkerala.org).

How to prepare

  • Travel time Kumarakom to Haripad ~1.5–2 hours by road. Google Maps route
  • Dress & gear Modest clothing (shoulders/knees covered), slip-on sandals for temple entry, small crossbody bag only — security checks are common
  • Photography Avoid flash near puja or kavadi bearers; ask before photographing devotees
  • Hearing protection Chenda melam can be very loud — foam earplugs are smart for longer sets
  • Hydration Bring sealed bottled water and small electrolyte sachets; February is warm and humid
  • Timing Arrive 30–45 minutes before processions to secure a viewing spot; crowds swell quickly
  • Parking & access Parking near the temple is limited; many visitors park at the bus stand or railway station and walk or taxi in (Holidify notes)
  • Cash & valuables Carry only what you need in a hidden pouch; dense festival crowds can attract pickpockets
  • Crowd flow Expect intermittent one-way closures around the temple as processions move — follow volunteer and police guidance
