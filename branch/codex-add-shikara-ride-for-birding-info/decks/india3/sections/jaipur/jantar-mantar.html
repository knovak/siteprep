Jantar Mantar (UNESCO Observatory)

Jaipur

Jantar Mantar is Jaipur's UNESCO-listed astronomical observatory with massive stone instruments still used to track celestial movements.

History & context

Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it is one of five observatories he commissioned across India.

What travelers like

  • Fascinating scale of astronomical instruments.
  • Guides help decode the functions.
  • UNESCO status and historical significance.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Limited shade in the open complex.
  • Can feel abstract without a guide.
  • Crowds during peak hours.
