Amsterdam

Amsterdam combines historic canal belts, major museums, bike-first mobility, and an active events calendar from late spring into summer. Visit Sunday May 31 to Tuesday June 2, sunrise 5:25am, sunset 9:51pm, full moon.

Basics for May 31–June 2

For May 31 to June 2, plan for typical late-spring to early-summer Amsterdam conditions: mild days, cooler evenings, and intermittent rain risk.

  • Municipality of Amsterdam: about 920,000–940,000 residents.
  • Metropolitan region: roughly 2.4–2.5 million residents.
  • Typical range: around 11–20°C.
  • Pack plan: light layers, comfortable walking shoes, and a compact waterproof shell.
  • What’s on listings: I amsterdam — What’s on.

Golden Age canals note

Amsterdam's "Golden Age canals" are the 17th-century Canal Ring, or Grachtengordel, laid out in concentric arcs around the old town during the Dutch Golden Age. UNESCO lists the canal-ring area inside the Singelgracht as a major urban-planning achievement, with drained swampland converted into canals, plots, merchant houses, bridges, and civic monuments.

  • Walk it: Start near Amsterdam Centraal or Dam Square, then loop along the three classic canals — Herengracht, Keizersgracht, and Prinsengracht — with a detour through the Jordaan and the Golden Bend on Herengracht if you want the grandest canal-house facades. A relaxed canal-ring walk is about 2 hours if you keep stops short.
  • Boat it: Standard canal cruises commonly depart near Prins Hendrikkade/Centraal Station, Damrak/Rokin, and Stadhouderskade near the Rijksmuseum; choose a route that explicitly includes Herengracht, Keizersgracht, and Prinsengracht if the canal houses are the priority.
  • Links: UNESCO Canal Ring listing | Amsterdam canal walking route | Canal cruise overview.

Trails

  • Amsterdamse Bos Loop: 4.5 stars, 4.8 mi, 262 ft gain, Difficulty: Easy. Flat forest-and-lake loop ideal for a quick half-day walk close to the city. AllTrails.
  • Waterleidingduinen Dune Trail: 4.5 stars, 5.9 mi, 187 ft gain, Difficulty: Moderate. Dune reserve trail with varied sandy terrain and frequent deer sightings west of Amsterdam. AllTrails.
  • Twiske Polder Circuit: 4.4 stars, 6.1 mi, 98 ft gain, Difficulty: Easy. Open polder paths and waterside sections with easy navigation and bike/train access. AllTrails.
  • Spaarnwoude Green Loop: 4.4 stars, 5.2 mi, 121 ft gain, Difficulty: Easy. Country-park style loop through meadows and woodlots, good for relaxed walking. AllTrails.
