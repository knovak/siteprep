Edinburgh

Edinburgh combines compact Old Town walking with major historical sights, rail connectivity, and strong food options within short distances. Visit Saturday June 6 to Saturday June 13, sunrise 4:31am, sunset 9:52pm, waning gibbous.

Expected weather for Edinburgh (June 8–10)

For early June in Edinburgh, expect mild days, cool evenings, and possible passing rain. Met Office long-term June averages at Edinburgh Gogarbank are around 17.5°C daytime highs, 9.4°C overnight lows, and about 69 mm monthly rainfall.

  • Pack plan: light rain shell, layers, and comfortable walking shoes for mixed pavement/cobbles.
  • Timing note: June daylight is long, so evenings are useful for additional walking plans.

Walking destinations

Edinburgh is especially good for walks that combine city texture with green corridors: riverside paths through former mill villages, short hill climbs for skyline views, and signed long-distance routes that can be sampled as day walks from town. A simple pattern is to do Arthur's Seat when you want the bigger hike, and switch to Calton Hill when you want an easier viewpoint walk.

  • Dean Village: A picturesque former milling village tucked into the Water of Leith valley, close to the West End and Stockbridge. It works well as a gentle photo-and-architecture stroll or as a scenic midpoint on a longer riverside walk along the Water of Leith Walkway. Google Maps.
  • Water of Leith Walkway: The main riverside walking spine through Edinburgh, running from the Shore at Leith toward Balerno and connecting Leith, Stockbridge, Dean Village, Murrayfield, Colinton, and the southwest edge of the city. The Water of Leith Conservation Trust notes useful section timings, including Balerno to Slateford at about 5 miles/2 hours and Slateford to Leith at about 7.5 miles/3 hours. Water of Leith Walkway guide | Google Maps.
  • Calton Hill: A short central climb at the east end of Princes Street with classic views across the Old Town, Arthur's Seat, the New Town, and the Firth of Forth; good at sunrise, sunset, or as a low-effort viewpoint between other central stops. If Arthur's Seat feels too long or weather-exposed, Calton Hill is the easier swap with great payoff. Google Maps.
  • John Muir Way from Edinburgh: The John Muir Way is a signed coast-to-coast route across central Scotland, running between Helensburgh and Dunbar. For an Edinburgh-based sample, use the official route maps to walk west from Edinburgh toward South Queensferry for Forth Bridge views, or east from Edinburgh toward Musselburgh, Aberlady, North Berwick, and eventually Dunbar, returning by train or bus instead of attempting the full route.
  • AllTrails top-rated walks near town: AllTrails' Edinburgh walking list is led by Arthur's Seat and related Holyrood Park routes, followed by nearby Pentlands options such as Pentland Ridge and Torduff Reservoir/Maiden's Cleugh. For a central itinerary, prioritize Arthur's Seat or the Dry Dam Path to Saint Anthony's Chapel; for a half-day hill outing, look south to the Pentlands; for a flatter urban walk, AllTrails also lists the Water of Leith Walkway and Leith-to-Dean-Village variants. Hiking Arthur's Seat

  • Arthur's Seat (Holyrood Park) — Edinburgh's iconic extinct-volcano summit with panoramic city and Firth of Forth views; popular routes start from Holyrood Park Road or Dunsapie Loch. Address: Queen's Drive, Holyrood Park, Edinburgh EH8 8HG, United Kingdom. Hours: Park open year-round; best in daylight with wind/rain checks. Holyrood Park visitor information | Google Maps.
  • Typical effort: Most direct climbs are around 45-90 minutes up (and less down), depending on route and weather; wear grippy shoes because paths can be muddy and rocky after rain.
