Overview

San Miguel de Allende

San Miguel de Allende is a highland city in eastern Guanajuato known for preserved colonial architecture, art schools and galleries, walkable historic streets, and the landmark pink towers of the Parroquia de San Miguel Arcángel; see this San Miguel de Allende overview for quick background.

Population

  • City/locality: about 72,000 residents in the 2020 census.
  • Municipality: about 160,000 residents in the 2020 census.

Brief history

The town began as a Spanish colonial settlement on routes linking central Mexico's mining and agricultural regions. It was originally associated with San Miguel el Grande and later renamed for Ignacio Allende, a local-born leader of Mexico's independence movement. Its historic center and nearby sanctuary area are recognized for Baroque/colonial urban fabric, civic plazas, churches, and the role the city played in independence-era history.

Climate for the first week of July

Early July is usually mild by day for central Mexico because of the city's highland setting, with the rainy season in progress. Expect pleasant mornings, warm afternoons, cool nights, and a meaningful chance of showers or thunderstorms; compare plans against San Miguel de Allende July climate averages.

  • Typical planning range: roughly 13-24°C / 56-75°F.
  • Rain plan: July is one of the wettest periods, so carry rain protection and use mornings for longer walks.
  • Comfort note: evenings can be cool after rain; pack a light layer.
