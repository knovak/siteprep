Oaxaca City

Oaxaca de Juárez is the capital of Oaxaca state: a walkable, food-focused highland city with Zapotec and Mixtec roots, Spanish-colonial architecture, major craft traditions, and quick access to the UNESCO-listed Monte Albán archaeological zone.

Basic Info

  • Travel dates: Sunday, July 12-Wednesday, July 15, 2026.
  • Population: about 259,000 residents in Oaxaca de Juárez municipality and about 714,000 in the metropolitan area.
  • Altitude and climate: the city center sits around 1,555 m / 5,102 ft. Oaxaca has a warm highland tropical savanna climate; July is rainy season, with comfortable mornings, warm afternoons, cool evenings, and frequent late-day showers or thunderstorms.
  • Expected July weather: plan around roughly 16-28°C / 60-82°F, with afternoon or evening rain. Schedule Monte Albán and walking tours early, carry a compact umbrella, and expect green hillsides.
  • Brief history: Spanish Oaxaca was formally founded in 1532, while the valley's much older urban history includes Monte Albán, a Zapotec capital founded around 500 BCE. The present historic center grew as a colonial administrative and trade city and is now recognized with Monte Albán as a UNESCO World Heritage area. See the Oaxaca City Wikipedia page for more background.
  • Holiday and festival timing: July is Guelaguetza season in Oaxaca. The closest regular observances are Our Lady of Mount Carmel / Virgen del Carmen on Thursday, July 16; the Benito Juárez death anniversary on Saturday, July 18; and the first 2026 Lunes del Cerro / Guelaguetza Monday on July 20, just after this stay. Expect cultural programming and higher hotel demand to build through mid-July.

Neighborhoods

  • Centro Histórico: the main visitor base around the Zócalo, Santo Domingo, museums, hotels, galleries, restaurants, mezcalerías, and pedestrian-friendly streets. Google Maps.
  • Jalatlaco: colorful, walkable former tannery neighborhood east of Centro, with murals, boutique stays, cafes, small restaurants, and a quieter evening feel. Google Maps.
  • Xochimilco: one of Oaxaca's oldest neighborhoods, known for aqueduct remnants, textiles, murals, family-run eateries, and a residential pace just north of the main core. Google Maps.
  • Reforma: more modern district north of Centro with restaurants, shops, and practical services; useful when Centro hotel rates spike during Guelaguetza season. Google Maps.

Attractions

  • Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzmán: ornate Baroque church and former Dominican complex anchoring the north side of Centro; pair it with the adjacent museum and ethnobotanical garden. Street address: C. Macedonio Alcalá s/n, RUTA INDEPENDENCIA, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: church generally daily; museum/garden schedules vary. Google Maps.
  • Museo de las Culturas de Oaxaca: regional history and culture museum in the former Santo Domingo monastery, with Mixtec, Zapotec, colonial, and post-independence collections. Street address: C. Macedonio Alcalá s/n, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: Tu-Su 10:00-17:00, M closed; verify before visiting. INAH information | Google Maps.
  • Jardín Etnobotánico de Oaxaca: guided-tour botanical garden preserving Oaxacan plants and cultural landscapes inside the Santo Domingo complex. Street address: Reforma Sur s/n, esquina Constitución, Col. Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: guided tours only; common Spanish tours M-Sa, English tours on select mornings, confirm current schedule. Official site | Google Maps.
  • Monte Albán Archaeological Zone: hilltop Zapotec capital with plazas, platforms, tombs, ball courts, and broad views over the Valley of Oaxaca. Street address: Zona Arqueológica de Monte Albán, Santa Cruz Xoxocotlán, Oaxaca, Mexico. Hours: generally daily 8:00-17:00; go early for heat, rain, and crowds. INAH information | Google Maps.
  • MACCO / Museo de Arte Contemporáneo y de las Culturas Oaxaqueñas: contemporary art and culture space on the pedestrian Andador Turístico, useful for a modern-art stop in the historic center. Street address: C. Macedonio Alcalá 202, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: verify current exhibition and opening schedule before going. Visit Mexico overview | Google Maps.
  • Hierve el Agua: mineral springs and petrified-waterfall formations in the mountains east of Oaxaca City; this is a longer half-day or full-day excursion, often paired with Mitla, Teotitlán del Valle, or mezcal stops. Street address: Hierve el Agua, San Lorenzo Albarradas, Oaxaca, Mexico. Hours: generally daytime access; confirm road and community access conditions. Google Maps.

Food

  • Mercado 20 de Noviembre: classic prepared-food market, especially the smoky grilled-meat corridor, tlayudas, chocolate, and busy lunch stalls. Street address: 20 de Noviembre 512, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: generally daily daytime into early evening. Google Maps.
  • Mercado Benito Juárez: central market for produce, mole pastes, chapulines, cheeses, textiles, and edible souvenirs. Street address: Las Casas s/n, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: generally daily daytime. Google Maps.
  • Boulenc: beloved bakery/cafe for breakfast, sandwiches, pastries, and coffee; go early or expect a wait. Street address: C. Porfirio Díaz 207, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: generally daily morning-evening; verify current hours. Google Maps.
  • Los Danzantes Oaxaca: polished contemporary Oaxacan restaurant near Santo Domingo with regional cooking, mezcal, and a dramatic open-air courtyard; reserve for dinner. Street address: C. Macedonio Alcalá 403 Interior 4, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Hours: daily 13:00-22:30; confirm before visiting. Website | Tripadvisor | Google Maps.

Events: July 12-15, 2026

Guelaguetza season build-up

  • Guelaguetza / Lunes del Cerro season: Sunday, July 12-Wednesday, July 15 falls in the festival build-up, before the major 2026 auditorium Mondays on July 20 and July 27. Watch the official program for calendas, concerts, craft fairs, mezcal and mole events, and neighborhood processions as July 16-20 approaches. Primary locations: Centro Histórico, Auditorio Guelaguetza, Alameda de León, and other city venues. Official Guelaguetza site | Auditorio map.
  • Virgen del Carmen vigils and neighborhood activity: Wednesday evening, July 15 is the eve of the July 16 feast day, so check churches and neighborhood plazas for processions, music, fireworks, or food stands. Location to check: Templo del Carmen Alto, García Vigil 600, Centro, 68000 Oaxaca de Juárez, Oax., Mexico. Google Maps.

Reliable anytime options during the stay

  • Andador Turístico evening walk: street musicians, gallery browsing, mezcal stops, and Santo Domingo/Zócalo strolling are strongest after late-afternoon rain clears. Location: C. Macedonio Alcalá between Santo Domingo and the Zócalo. Google Maps.
  • Oaxaca craft village day trips: if city events are thin before July 16, use one day for Teotitlán del Valle weaving, San Bartolo Coyotepec black pottery, or San Martín Tilcajete alebrijes. Teotitlán map | San Bartolo map | Tilcajete map.
