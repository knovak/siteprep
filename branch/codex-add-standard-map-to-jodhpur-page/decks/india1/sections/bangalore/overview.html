Overview

Bangalore (Bengaluru)

Bangalore is Karnataka's tech-forward capital, blending start-up energy with leafy parks, heritage architecture, and a thriving dining scene. The city's elevation keeps temperatures moderate year-round.

Population: ~12.8 million (metro)
Typical January weather: 15–27°C, dry mornings and cool nights
Typical February weather: 17–30°C, warm afternoons, low humidity

Fast facts

  • Key areas: Central Business District, Indiranagar, Koramangala, Whitefield.
  • Transit: Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) lies ~35 km north; Namma Metro connects MG Road, Majestic, and Whitefield.
  • Green escapes: Cubbon Park and Lalbagh Botanical Garden for morning walks and runs.
  • Food notes: Filter coffee, dosa houses, and a growing modern Indian and craft beer scene.

Top Bangalore-area attractions

  • Lalbagh Botanical Garden: A 240-acre historic garden with glasshouse flower shows, walking paths, and rare plant collections.
  • Cubbon Park: A central green lung framed by museums and heritage buildings, popular for early-morning walks and picnics.
  • Bangalore Palace: Tudor-style palace tours highlight ornate interiors, royal memorabilia, and landscaped grounds.
  • ISKCON Bangalore: A major Krishna temple complex with cultural programs, a vegetarian food court, and skyline views.
  • 108 Shivlinga Temple (Shivlinga): A serene hilltop shrine near the Art of Living campus known for its ring of 108 Shiva lingams.
  • Gaushala (Gashalla): The Art of Living cow shelter offers a quiet visit to learn about conservation, care, and daily rituals.
  • Nandi Hills: A sunrise viewpoint and quick getaway with fort ruins and cool, breezy walks an hour from the city.

Mysore quick notes

  • Travel time: ~3 to 3.5 hours by road from Bangalore (roughly 145 km), with frequent trains and buses.
  • Top attractions: Mysore Palace, Chamundi Hill, Brindavan Gardens, and the Devaraja Market for silk, spices, and sweets.
