Luggage Handling

For rail-station storage, Excess Baggage / Left Baggage currently lists booking locations at London Bridge, London Charing Cross, London Euston, London King’s Cross, London Liverpool Street, London Paddington, London St Pancras International, London Victoria, London Waterloo, Birmingham New Street, Edinburgh Waverley, Glasgow Central, Leeds, Liverpool Lime Street, and Manchester Piccadilly. Their separate rail-station store page lists the London stations as Charing Cross, Euston, King’s Cross, Liverpool Street, Paddington, St Pancras International, Victoria, Waterloo, and London Bridge.

  • Rail-station storage links: Left Baggage booking list and Excess Baggage Company rail-station stores.
  • UK luggage couriers: Services such as Send My Bag, My Baggage, and Luggage Forward can move bags across Scotland and elsewhere in the UK between hotels or other addresses.
  • How courier services work: Book online, enter pickup and delivery addresses, and use hotels where appropriate; bags are collected by courier, often DHL or UPS, with mainland UK delivery commonly next day or within two days.
  • Service notes: Send My Bag is a UK-founded door-to-door provider with online quotes, shipment options, tracking, and a focus on traveling lighter. My Baggage provides online booking and tracking for worldwide collection/delivery, often through major couriers and especially for students or movers. Luggage Forward is a premium door-to-door option for 200+ countries with live tracking and on-time delivery choices.
  • Flexible local moves: Marketplaces like Shiply or AnyVan connect travelers with local couriers who bid on bespoke routes, including hotel-to-hotel and possible same-day jobs.
  • Trail baggage services: Dedicated regional operators, such as Highland or West Highland baggage walkers on popular multi-day trails, may offer fixed per-bag pricing.

Mass Transit

  • TfL Pay-As-You-Go in London/southeast: Tap in and out with the same contactless card/device across Tube, buses, DLR, Overground, and trams.
  • Fare capping: Daily and weekly capping applies when you use one consistent payment method.
  • Outside core contactless area: On many National Rail journeys beyond the capping/contactless zone, contactless PAYG is not always available or guaranteed.
  • Tickets still needed for some journeys: Advance/Anytime eTickets or rail operator tickets (for example, GWR) are often required outside the core network.
  • Railcard note: Railcard discounts do not always apply to contactless fares.
  • Touch rules: London buses/trams are touch-in only, while Tube and most rail journeys require touch-in and touch-out with the same card/device to avoid a maximum fare charge.
