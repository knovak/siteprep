Overview

Brisbane

Queensland's riverfront capital mixes subtropical weather with a busy performing arts calendar, making March a lively time for theatre, live music, and festival programming.

Typical March weather: 21–29°C, humid days with afternoon storms
What to pack: Lightweight layers, breathable fabrics, and a compact rain jacket
Getting around: CityCat ferries, buses, and trains connect South Bank and Fortitude Valley

QPAC highlights in March

  • Queensland Symphony Orchestra: Seasonal orchestral concerts in the Concert Hall.
  • Touring musicals: Check the Lyric Theatre calendar for visiting productions.
  • Dance and cabaret: Concert Hall and Playhouse programs often feature contemporary dance and vocal showcases.

Other Brisbane venues to watch

  • Brisbane Powerhouse: Music residencies, comedy nights, and performance art in New Farm.
  • The Tivoli & Fortitude Music Hall: Touring bands and international DJ sets in Fortitude Valley.
  • QPAC's Cremorne Theatre: Intimate theatre and spoken-word performances.
  • Riverstage: Outdoor concerts and special events in the City Botanic Gardens.

March planning tips

  • Expect evening showers; allow extra travel time between venues.
  • Secure tickets early for headline touring shows and orchestra programs.
  • Pair nights out with South Bank dining or a post-show walk along the river.
