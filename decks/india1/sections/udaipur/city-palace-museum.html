Attraction

City Palace & City Palace Museum

Udaipur

The City Palace complex crowns Udaipur's lakefront and is the largest palace complex in Rajasthan, with museums spread across courtyards, balconies, and galleries overlooking Lake Pichola.

History & context

Construction began in the 16th century under Maharana Udai Singh II and continued for nearly 400 years as successive Mewar rulers expanded the palace and added museums, courtyards, and ceremonial spaces.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Typically 9:30 AM–5:30 PM; some galleries and special exhibits keep separate timings.
  • Address: City Palace Road, Old City, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001.
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: In the old city lakefront area (0–1 km from Clock Tower and the main bazaar).

What travelers like

  • Lake-facing balconies and panoramic views of Lake Pichola.
  • Richer museum collections (arms, textiles, miniatures) than most Rajasthan city palaces.
  • Grand courtyards and the Crystal Gallery (ticketed separately).

What travelers dislike or note

  • Multiple ticket categories and add-on fees can feel confusing.
  • Crowded corridors at peak hours.
  • Limited shade in outer courtyards midday.
