Attraction

Sajjangarh / Monsoon Palace

Udaipur

Perched on a hilltop, the Monsoon Palace offers sweeping views of Udaipur's lakes and the Aravalli range, especially dramatic at sunset.

History & context

Built in 1884 by Maharana Sajjan Singh, the palace was intended as a monsoon retreat and astronomical center, later used as a royal hunting lodge.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Typically 9:00 AM–6:00 PM; last entry may be before sunset.
  • Address: Sajjangarh, Badi Road, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001.
  • Google Map: Google Map
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: Roughly 6–7 km from the old city (20–30 minutes by car).

What travelers like

  • Panoramic sunset views over lakes and hills.
  • Cooler breezes on the hilltop.
  • Great spot for landscape photography.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Interior rooms are sparse compared to city palaces.
  • Steep, winding approach road.
  • Can be crowded near sunset.
