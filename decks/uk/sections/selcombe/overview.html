Overview

Selcombe (Salcombe)

Salcombe (often misspelled as “Selcombe”) is a picturesque South Hams harbour town in Devon, set on the Kingsbridge Estuary and known for sailing, estuary views, and access to South West Coast Path beaches.

Location Snapshot

  • Name: Salcombe, South Hams, Devon, England.
  • Brief summary: A compact estuary-side resort town with steep lanes, ferries across the water, nearby coves, and strong summer tourism demand.
  • Street address (civic anchor): Salcombe Town Council, Cliff House, Cliff Road, Salcombe, Devon TQ8 8JQ, United Kingdom.
  • Google Map: Salcombe on Google Maps.
  • Hours: Town area is always open; specific venue, ferry, and shop hours vary seasonally.

Planning Notes

  • Use Salcombe as a base for short coastal walks, beach days, and estuary ferry hops to East Portlemouth beaches.
  • Roads into town are narrow and parking is limited in peak season; reserve parking and dining early for weekends.
  • Expect the busiest conditions in summer and school holidays; shoulder seasons are calmer for walking and dining.

Travel from London

  • Most rail itineraries from London use Totnes as the hub station for South Devon connections, with onward travel to Salcombe by bus or taxi.
  • The Tally Ho! 164 bus links Totnes rail station to Salcombe via Kingsbridge, usually around hourly by day; Sundays have fewer departures (typically around midday, mid-afternoon, and early evening), so connection timing matters.
  • Typical Totnes → Salcombe public-transport time is around 50–60 minutes by bus; later arrivals often need a taxi because evening service is limited.
  • Taxi fallback from Totnes is generally faster (around 28–40 minutes, traffic dependent) and is commonly priced around £45–£65 from the station area to Salcombe.
  • For same-day return travel, check whether a day ticket is offered on local bus services, as it can be cheaper than buying two singles.
  • For late flights or tight airport connections, consider a prebooked private transfer direct to Salcombe (often used when trains/buses have ended), and include a 60–90 minute airport buffer for immigration and baggage before pickup.
