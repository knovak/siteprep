Salcombe

Salcombe (often misspelled as “Selcombe”) is a picturesque South Hams harbour town in Devon, set on the Kingsbridge Estuary and known for sailing, estuary views, and access to South West Coast Path beaches.

Planning Notes

  • Use Salcombe as a base for short coastal walks, beach days, and estuary ferry hops to East Portlemouth beaches.
  • Roads into town are narrow and parking is limited in peak season; reserve parking and dining early for weekends.
  • Expect the busiest conditions in summer and school holidays; shoulder seasons are calmer for walking and dining.

Ferries around Salcombe

  • Salcombe ↔ South Sands: The passenger ferry generally runs at roughly half-hour intervals in warmer months, usually starting around 09:45 from Whitestrand (Salcombe) and around 10:00 from South Sands, continuing into mid/late afternoon. The published season is typically late March through late October, but timing and operation can shift with sea and weather conditions.
  • Kingsbridge ↔ Salcombe: This ferry is tide-led, so departures vary by day rather than following a fixed clock schedule. Some sailings may use alternate quays when water levels require it, so checking the latest operator timetable before travel is important.
  • Dartmouth ↔ Kingswear services: Lower Ferry, Higher Ferry, and passenger services on the Dart generally run across longer operating windows (early morning to late evening), but remain tide/weather dependent, and some services use contactless-only payment.
  • Practical tip: Across all routes, always re-check operator notices on the day for departure points, exact sailings, and payment options.

Travel from London

  • Most rail itineraries from London use Totnes as the hub station for South Devon connections, with onward travel to Salcombe by bus or taxi.
  • The Tally Ho! 164 bus links Totnes rail station to Salcombe via Kingsbridge, usually around hourly by day; Sundays have fewer departures (typically around midday, mid-afternoon, and early evening), so connection timing matters.
  • Typical Totnes → Salcombe public-transport time is around 50–60 minutes by bus; later arrivals often need a taxi because evening service is limited.
  • Taxi fallback from Totnes is generally faster (around 28–40 minutes, traffic dependent) and is commonly priced around £45–£65 from the station area to Salcombe.
  • For same-day return travel, check whether a day ticket is offered on local bus services, as it can be cheaper than buying two singles.
  • For late flights or tight airport connections, consider a prebooked private transfer direct to Salcombe (often used when trains/buses have ended), and include a 60–90 minute airport buffer for immigration and baggage before pickup.

Travel from Exeter

  • The standard public-transport chain is Exeter Airport → Exeter St Davids → Totnes → Salcombe, with a Stagecoach airport bus, a mainline train, then the Tally Ho! 164 bus.
  • Exeter Airport states that Stagecoach 4A connects the airport with Exeter St Davids (and Exeter Central), and also flags reduced weekend/bank-holiday frequency, so always check same-day times before booking tight rail connections.
  • From Exeter St Davids, use a direct train toward Totnes, then connect to the Tally Ho! 164 from Totnes rail station forecourt to Salcombe via Kingsbridge.
  • The 164 timetable shows frequent daytime departures on weekdays and Saturdays, with fewer departures on Sundays/holidays; plan extra buffer if your flight lands later in the day.
  • If the final bus connection is missed, Exeter Airport’s official taxi partner is Apple Taxis; prebooking a through transfer (or at least Totnes ↔ Salcombe backup) is the safer option for evening arrivals.
  • For live planning, use Traveline or National Rail journey planners on the day of travel, since rail engineering works and seasonal bus updates can shift connections.

Local Taxis

For South Hams transfers, pre-booking is usually the safest option for fixed pricing and availability, especially for Totnes rail arrivals, evening returns, and airport runs.

  • Station Cabs Totnes – established Totnes operator serving Totnes, Salcombe, Kingsbridge, Dartmouth, and airport/rail transfers; published fare guides often place Totnes → Salcombe around £60–£65 and Dartmouth/Kingsbridge trips around £40–£45.
  • South Hams Cabs – Totnes-based service covering South Hams local and long-distance private hire, including station and airport transfers with multi-seat options.
  • Merlin's Taxi and private hire – Kingsbridge/Totnes area operator for local pickups, drop-offs, and longer trips including Exeter Airport, with phone and online booking.
  • 24/7 Taxis Kingsbridge & Salcombe LTD – 24-hour coverage for Kingsbridge, Salcombe, and nearby villages.

Other local options often used for short trips include Twenty20taxis, HR PRIVATE HIRE TAXI KINGSBRIDGE & SALCOMBE Ltd, One 2 One Taxis, and Totally Taxis Salcombe.

Ballpark fares (confirm at booking): Kingsbridge ↔ Salcombe local hops are often in the mid-teens for short runs; Totnes ↔ Salcombe is commonly around £60–£65 private hire; Exeter Airport ↔ Salcombe (roughly 70–80 miles) is often closer to £80–£110 depending on timing and vehicle.

Burgh Island

Burgh Island sits off South Devon near Bigbury-on-Sea and is linked to the mainland by a tidal sandy causeway that is exposed at low water and submerged at high tide (see Wikipedia background).

  • Name: Burgh Island, Bigbury-on-Sea, South Devon, England.
  • Brief summary: A small tidal island known for dramatic coastal views, the Art Deco Burgh Island Hotel, and the historic Pilchard Inn.
  • Street address: Burgh Island Hotel, Burgh Island, Bigbury-on-Sea, Kingsbridge, Devon TQ7 4BG, United Kingdom.
  • Google Map: Burgh Island on Google Maps.
  • Hours: Island access varies by tide; the causeway is walkable around low tide only, while Sea Tractor operation is tide/weather dependent.

At low tide, visitors can usually walk the causeway for a limited tidal window (Tripadvisor discussion: causeway access thread). When the tide is in, transfer is typically by Burgh Island’s iconic Sea Tractor “Trundle” (official Sea Tractor information), generally with hotel guests prioritized and day visitors advised to check ahead.

On-island highlights include the Art Deco hotel, the Pilchard Inn, and short scenic coastal walks; for regional visitor context see Visit South Devon.
