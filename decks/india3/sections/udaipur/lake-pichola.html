Lake Pichola

Udaipur

Lake Pichola is the iconic artificial lake at Udaipur's heart, flanked by palaces, ghats, and havelis that light up at sunset.

History & context

The lake was created in 1362 by a local farmer (Pichola) and later enlarged by the Mewar rulers, who added embankments, ghats, and palace islands.

Visitor details

  • Hours: Open all day; boat activity is typically morning through sunset.
  • Address: Lake Pichola, Old City lakefront, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001.
  • Google Map: Google Map
  • Distance from Udaipur city center: Central to the old city; ghats are within 0–1 km of the Clock Tower area.

What travelers like

  • Sunset reflections of the City Palace and Lake Palace.
  • Walking along Lal Ghat and Gangaur Ghat.
  • Photo-friendly lakefront cafés and viewpoints.

What travelers dislike or note

  • Seasonal water levels can expose muddy edges in dry months.
  • Boat queues and touts around prime sunset time.
  • Litter can be visible around some ghats.
