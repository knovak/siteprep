Overview

Jaipur

Jaipur, the Pink City, anchors Rajasthan's Golden Triangle with palaces, planned boulevards, and lively bazaars. Its winter climate makes for comfortable fort visits and walking tours.

Population: ~4.0 million (metro)
Typical January weather: 8–23°C, cool mornings
Typical February weather: 11–27°C, sunny and dry

Fast facts

  • Highlights: Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal, Jantar Mantar, and Nahargarh sunset views.
  • Markets: Johari Bazaar for jewelry, Bapu Bazaar for textiles, and plenty of lassi stops.
  • Transit: Jaipur International Airport (JAI) is ~12 km from the city center; metro and ride-hailing cover inner-city travel.
  • Day trips: Chand Baori stepwell and Sanganer block-print studios are popular add-ons.

Festival spotlight

Nagaur Cattle Fair (Feb 17–21, 2026) is held in Nagaur between Jodhpur and Bikaner and is Rajasthan’s second-largest livestock fair. Expect bustling animal trading with tens of thousands of bullocks, camels, horses, and oxen, plus folk performances and rural culture showcases that feel distinctly local compared to more touristy fairs.
