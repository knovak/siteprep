Warsaw

Warsaw blends rebuilt historic districts with broad boulevards, modern transit, and an active everyday food scene.

Expected weather for May 20–24

For May 20–24 in Warsaw, plan for late-spring conditions with mild to warm afternoons and cooler evenings. Typical May climate values in Warsaw are near 19°C daytime highs, 8°C nighttime lows, and about 55 mm rainfall across the month.

  • Pack plan: light layers, a compact rain shell, and comfortable walking shoes.
  • Practical note: daily conditions can swing between sunny and wet in May, so check a short-range forecast again 2-3 days before travel.

Milk Bars

Polish bar mleczny (milk bars) began in the late 19th century and expanded widely in the communist era as low-cost, cafeteria-style canteens. Today they remain a popular way to eat simple traditional Polish dishes at budget-friendly prices.

  • Bar Mleczny Bambino — One of Warsaw's classic milk bars (operating since 1959).

POLIN Museum and Royal Castle ticketing strategy (example: Thursday, 21 May)

  • POLIN free-admission pattern: On days like Thursday, 21 May, POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews can offer free admission for both the core and temporary exhibitions across the day.
  • Important cutoff times: Even on free days, plan around last-entry cutoffs of roughly 16:00 for the core exhibition and around 17:30 for temporary shows.
  • Crowd behavior: Late afternoon often gets busier as visitors rush to enter before final admission windows.
  • Queue-avoidance tip: Reserving in advance or booking a guided tour can improve your chance of entering without long waits.
  • Royal Castle system: The Royal Castle in Warsaw generally runs a paid-ticket system for State Rooms and temporary exhibitions, with concerts/evening programs sold through its own box office or mainstream ticket sellers.
  • Best practice: Buy Royal Castle tickets online ahead of time whenever possible to reduce queue risk and avoid sold-out time slots.
