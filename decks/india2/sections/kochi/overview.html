Port city on the Malabar Coast

Kochi (Cochin) is a port city in Kerala known for its backwaters, spice trade legacy, and blend of Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences visible around Fort Kochi and Mattancherry.

Fast facts

  • Highlights Chinese fishing nets, St. Francis Church, Jew Town and the Paradesi Synagogue, and Kathakali performances
  • Backwaters Day cruises to Vembanad Lake and routes toward Alleppey start from nearby jetties
  • Transit Cochin International Airport (COK) ~35 km northeast; metro and ferries connect Ernakulam and Fort Kochi
  • Food Seafood, appam with stew, and Kerala-style biryani headline local cuisine
  • Population ~0.7 million (city proper)

Neighborhoods

Fort Kochi

Flat, compact, and genuinely walkable, Fort Kochi packs historic churches, colonial-era streets, cafés, and galleries into a relaxed, photogenic core. It's the easiest base for first-time visitors who want to stroll between key sights without traffic stress.

Mattancherry

Adjacent to Fort Kochi and rich with Jewish, Arab, Portuguese, and Dutch history, Mattancherry is best for daytime walking around the Paradesi Synagogue, Dutch Palace, and spice warehouses. It's more lived-in and less café-oriented, but still walkable and rewarding for history lovers.

Jew Town (Mattancherry)

A short, intensely historic lane filled with antiques, spice shops, and heritage homes. It's very walkable, usually a 1–2 hour wander, and feels most pleasant in the early morning before midday crowds.

Vypeen (Vypin) Island

Reached by ferry from Fort Kochi, Vypeen offers quiet village streets, fishing communities, and long waterfront walks. Ideal for a slower pace and sunset strolls, though you'll need to time ferries and accept fewer headline monuments.

Marine Drive (Mainland)

A waterfront promenade on the mainland that's easy to walk and scenic in the evening. More modern and less historic — plan on transport if you want to connect back to the heritage quarters.

Waterway rides solar ferry + Water Metro

Kerala's backwaters now offer easy, scenic water transport — from a pioneering solar ferry on Vembanad Lake to Kochi's electric Water Metro routes around the islands and mainland hubs.

  • Aditya solar ferry India's first solar-powered passenger boat — Vaikom ↔ Thavanakkadavu, 2.5 km on Vembanad Lake, ~15 minutes, near-silent since 2017
  • Kochi Water Metro Electric boats connecting Vyttila, Fort Kochi, Vypin, and island terminals with frequent, scheduled sailings
  • Easy ticketing Commuter-friendly fares; Kochi1 prepaid options enable a 1–3 hour hop-on loop between islands and mainland stops
