Overview

Mexico City

Mexico City is Mexico's capital, a high-elevation metropolis in the Valley of Mexico with dense neighborhoods, major museums, layered Indigenous/colonial/modern history, and a strong food and arts scene.

Population

  • City proper / federal entity: about 9.21 million residents in the 2020 census.
  • Greater Mexico City: about 21.8 million residents in the officially defined metropolitan area.

Brief history

The city stands on and around the former Mexica capital of Tenochtitlan, founded on Lake Texcoco and conquered by Spanish forces in 1521. The Spanish colonial capital was then built over the Indigenous city, making the present historic center a layered landscape of pre-Hispanic ruins, colonial churches and palaces, republican institutions, and modern urban expansion. In the 20th and 21st centuries, Mexico City became the country's political, cultural, educational, and economic hub.

Climate for the first week of July

Early July is in the wet season. Expect mild-to-warm days, cool evenings, frequent cloud build-up, and a good chance of afternoon or evening showers or thunderstorms.

  • Typical planning range: roughly 13-24°C / 55-75°F.
  • Rain plan: carry a compact umbrella or rain shell, and schedule outdoor walking for mornings when possible.
  • Altitude note: the city sits at high elevation, so sun can feel strong even on mild days.
