Overview

Vilnius

Vilnius has broad transport coverage, with both app-based rides and public transport making it easy to move between the airport and city neighborhoods.

Taxis and ride-hailing

  • Ride-hailing is widely available: Bolt is the most widely used app in Vilnius, Uber is also available, and local apps such as eTAKSI operate in the city.
  • Airport pickups are supported: At Vilnius Airport, Bolt operates with dedicated pickup zones, and ride-hailing pickups are permitted.
  • Traditional taxis still operate: You can use official taxi ranks and generally pay by card or cash.
  • Why apps help: Booking through apps typically shows the fare upfront, which helps avoid price surprises.

Public transport

  • City network: Buses and trolleybuses provide broad, efficient city coverage.
  • Ticket card: The Vilniečio kortelė is a reloadable e-ticket card that you tap when boarding.
  • Mobile ticketing and planning: Apps such as m.Ticket, Trafi, and Judu let you plan routes and buy digital tickets on the go.
  • Metro status: Vilnius does not currently have a metro in service.

Vilnius → Riga by bus or train

  • FlixBus: FlixBus runs Vilnius-Riga coach services and is usually the easiest place to compare departure times and fares in one view.
  • Other direct coach options: You can also compare schedules and buy tickets with Lux Express and Ecolines, both of which regularly operate Baltics intercity routes.
  • Rail option: For train travel, check LTG Link (Lithuania) together with Vivi (Latvia) for current Vilnius-Riga routing and ticket details.
  • Planning tip: Book earlier for Friday/Sunday departures, and compare both bus and rail because total trip time can vary by connection design and season.

Hot-air ballooning near Vilnius and Trakai

  • Why it's special: Go Vilnius promotes ballooning over the city/Old Town area as a signature warm-season activity.
  • Seasonality: Main city season is commonly mid-April to mid-October; flights are weather-dependent and confirmed close to departure.
  • Typical timings: Sunrise and pre-sunset departures are standard windows for safer, calmer wind profiles.
  • Price range: Operator listings on Ballooning.lt and Balloon.lt commonly show shared flights around €140-€150 per person, with private flights priced higher.
  • Vilnius vs Trakai: Both are offered by local operators; Trakai routes are popular for lake-and-castle views.
  • How to book: Directly with operators (e.g., Ballooning.lt and Balloon.lt/HotAirBalloon.lt) or through OTA listings such as Viator.

Vilnius → Trakai day-trip quick plan

  • Distance: Trakai is roughly 29 km from central Vilnius.
  • Train option: LTG Link schedules show typical journey times around 32-35 minutes (depending on departure).
  • Bus option: Buses also run frequently from Vilnius bus station; exact headways vary by day and season.
  • Last-mile on arrival: Plan a 20-35 minute walk from station areas to the island-castle side, or use local taxi/bus for speed.
  • Main sight: Trakai Island Castle (museum complex) on Lake Galvė, with published seasonal ticket tables and multi-site options.
  • Early-start benefit: Earlier departures help with crowds, photography light, and flexible return timing.

MO Museum tickets and visit planning

  • Timed e-tickets: MO Museum sells e-tickets through its official portal and ties each booking to a specific date and time selected at checkout. Ticket purchase rules
  • Delivery and entry: After payment, e-tickets are sent by email with a QR code; you can present them on your phone or as a printout at entry. Ticket purchase rules
  • Cutoff timing: Last tickets are sold up to 30 minutes before closing in both the online shop and on-site ticket office. For your visit
  • Strict terms: Tickets are generally non-exchangeable and non-refundable except when the visit was impossible or an event was cancelled by MO Museum. Ticket purchase rules
  • Booking strategy: Entry usually does not require separate pre-registration, but booking timed slots early is recommended for busy dates. MO FAQ
  • On-site experience options: MO offers a free e-guide and separately bookable guided tours (including some English-language options). MO FAQ
  • Flexibility check: If flexible cancellation matters, confirm current return/refund terms before purchasing at mo@mo.lt. Ticket purchase rules

Vilnius Cathedral crypts and bell tower access

  • Access model: Cathedral crypts and bell tower access are typically not casual walk-ins; the Church Heritage Museum runs limited guided/ticketed access when events are published. What’s on: guided crypt tour
  • Museum role: The Church Heritage Museum (in St Michael’s Church) preserves major cathedral heritage collections and organizes occasional tours to the crypts and bell tower. Church Heritage Museum
  • Crypt tour logistics: Crypt tours are listed as discrete calendar events, usually at specific times with small groups, and pre-registration by phone/email is mandatory. What’s on: guided crypt tour
  • What crypt access includes: Guided entry covers burial chambers, royal tombs, and archaeological spaces beneath the cathedral. Cathedral crypts overview
  • Bell tower/combo products: Bell tower visits and bundled products (treasury + bell tower + crypts) are available through scheduled products and tour formats. Example combo listing
  • Best booking approach: Monitor the museum event calendar and contact the museum quickly when dates open; confirm change/refund terms when booking. What’s on: guided crypt tour
